Identity Chooser

Welcome to version 4 of Identity Chooser.

Do you remember the first time you accidentally sent a work related email from your private email account? Identity Chooser helps you to prevent that. It makes choosing the sender address explicit:

  • Do you want to compose a new email? Identity Chooser forces you to choose the sender address first.
  • Do you want to forward an email? Identity Chooser forces you to choose the sender address first.
  • To make it short: Every time you'll create a new message, Identity Chooser shows you a menu with all your identities.

Everything in Identity Chooser is about making explicit choices. No automatisms, no defaults.

How does it work?

Whenever you open an email editor -- be it to write a new email, reply to one or forward it -- Identity Chooser will open a window to force you to choose your sender address before it let's you access the email editor.

This new workflow has the advantage that it works every time and everywhere Thunderbird opens a new message. This means, Identity Chooser doesn't only work in the main window; it also is active when you create a new email directly out of the address book, for instance.

Configuration

Identity Chooser is very configurable:

  • It let's you decide when it is active (write, reply and/or forward),
  • It allows you to hide emails, and
  • It let's you rearrange the order of your email adresses

If you want to show a colorful border around the "+ New Message" window, so you can tell different identities apart, I recommend the addon Border Colors D. Identity Chooser detects an installed copy of Border Colors D and integrates with it intelligently.
